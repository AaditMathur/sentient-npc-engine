# 🏆 Sentient NPC Engine 3.0 - Hackathon Submission

## Executive Summary

We've built a production-grade AI middleware that transforms game NPCs from scripted automatons into truly sentient, autonomous agents with emergent behaviors. This isn't just another chatbot - it's a complete cognitive architecture that creates living, breathing game worlds.

## 🎯 The Problem

Traditional game NPCs are:
- Scripted and predictable
- Don't remember past interactions
- Have no emotional depth
- Can't form relationships
- Don't react to world events
- Create static, lifeless worlds

## 💡 Our Solution

A complete cognitive architecture with 7 groundbreaking innovation features that create emergent, dynamic game worlds.

---

## 🚀 Innovation Features

### 1. 🗣️ Multi-NPC Conversations
**What it does:** NPCs autonomously talk to each other without player involvement

**Why it's innovative:**
- Creates emergent storylines
- Gossip spreads naturally through social networks
- Conversations influenced by personality, emotions, relationships
- No scripting required - fully autonomous

**Technical approach:**
- LLM-powered dialogue generation
- Social graph analysis for conversation triggers
- Personality and emotion modulation
- Multi-turn conversation orchestration

**Demo:** Two NPCs discussing a recent crime, forming opinions, and spreading rumors

---

### 2. 😱 Emotional Contagion
**What it does:** Emotions spread through crowds like real social contagion

**Why it's innovative:**
- Based on real social psychology research
- Panic cascades during attacks
- Joy spreads at festivals
- Crowd amplification effects
- Personality-based resistance

**Technical approach:**
- Social contagion models
- Distance decay functions
- Personality modulation (empathy increases susceptibility)
- Relationship amplification (trust increases contagion)

**Demo:** Dragon attack causes panic that spreads to 88% of NPCs in the area

---

### 3. ⚔️ Dynamic Quest Generation
**What it does:** NPCs autonomously create quests based on their experiences

**Why it's innovative:**
- No manual quest design needed
- Quests emerge from NPC goals and emotions
- Crime victims generate revenge quests
- Merchants create retrieval quests
- Rewards scale with emotional intensity

**Technical approach:**
- Goal-based quest templates
- Emotion-driven urgency calculation
- Personality-influenced quest types
- Automatic reward scaling

**Demo:** Merchant whose goods were stolen automatically generates a bounty quest

---

### 4. ⚡ Temporal Causality Chains
**What it does:** Tracks cause-and-effect relationships across the entire world

**Why it's innovative:**
- Visualizes butterfly effects
- Predicts future consequences
- Traces events back to root causes
- Measures player impact
- Creates narrative coherence

**Technical approach:**
- Directed acyclic graph (DAG) of causal relationships
- Butterfly effect scoring algorithm
- Consequence prediction using historical patterns
- Chain depth and amplification metrics

**Demo:** Small theft cascades into town-wide panic, affecting 45 NPCs across 7 degrees of separation

---

### 5. 📜 Cultural Memory & Legends
**What it does:** Events become folklore, player reputation becomes legendary

**Why it's innovative:**
- Collective NPC memory
- Legends evolve through retelling
- Player reputation persists and grows
- Cultural narratives shape behavior
- Fidelity decay (details become fuzzy)

**Technical approach:**
- Legend status progression (rumor → story → folklore → legend → myth)
- Embellishment system
- Spread tracking through social networks
- Reputation calculation
- Persistence decay

**Demo:** Player's heist becomes "The Great Heist of Shadow Thief" - a legend known by 45 NPCs

---

### 6. 💭 Dream & Subconscious System
**What it does:** NPCs dream and process memories, influencing behavior

**Why it's innovative:**
- Psychological realism
- Trauma processing (nightmares reduce fear)
- Prophetic dreams hint at future
- Dreams inspire new goals
- Influences next-day behavior

**Technical approach:**
- Dream type classification (nightmare, prophetic, wish-fulfillment, etc.)
- Memory-emotion-goal integration
- Symbolism library
- Emotion impact calculation
- Behavior modification

**Demo:** NPC has nightmare about recent attack, processes trauma, wakes up cautious but less afraid

---

### 7. 📊 Real-Time Analytics Dashboard
**What it does:** Visualizes world state, emotions, and causality in real-time

**Why it's innovative:**

- Real-time emotion heatmaps

## 🎨 Technical Architecture

### Core Systems
- **LLM Backend:** Anthropic Claude (claude-sonnet-4-20250514)
- **Vector Memory:** Qdrant for semantic memory search
- **Social Graph:** Neo4j for relationship tracking
- **Event Streaming:** Redis Streams for world events
- **State Management:** PostgreSQL + SQLAlchemy
- **API:** FastAPI with async/await

### Innovation Systems
- **Conversation Engine:** Multi-turn autonomous dialogue
- **Contagion Engine:** Social physics simulation
- **Quest Generator:** Template-based dynamic generation
- **Causality Tracker:** DAG-based event tracking
- **Legend System:** Cultural memory evolution
- **Dream Engine:** Psychological simulation
- **Analytics Dashboard:** Real-time visualization

## 🎮 Demo Scenarios

### Scenario 1: The Butterfly Effect
1. Player steals 50 gold from merchant
2. Merchant generates bounty quest
3. Panic spreads through market (emotional contagion)
4. Guards increase patrols (NPC decision)
5. Other merchants raise prices (economic impact)
6. Event becomes legend: "The Great Market Heist"
7. Player reputation becomes "villain" (-0.75)

**Result:** One small action affects 45 NPCs across 7 degrees of separation

### Scenario 2: Emergent Drama
1. Two NPCs start conversation about recent crime
2. One NPC is friend of victim, becomes angry
3. Anger spreads to nearby NPCs (contagion)
4. Multiple NPCs generate revenge quests
5. Story spreads as legend
6. NPCs reference legend in future conversations

**Result:** Autonomous storyline with no scripting

### Scenario 3: Psychological Realism
1. NPC witnesses traumatic event
2. High fear emotion state
3. That night, NPC has nightmare
4. Nightmare processes trauma (fear reduced)
5. Next day, NPC is cautious but functional
6. Memory importance decays over time

**Result:** Realistic psychological processing
